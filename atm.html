The ATM Program

The ATM Program is a computer-technology elective at Hubert H. Bancroft Middle School. The three year program focuses on teaching students the basics of computer technology and its programs. Students learn a plethora of things from typing, coding, filmmaking, floorplanning, and graphic design. The program is incredibly fun!

6th Grade ATM

The 6th grade marks the start of the ATM program. In Year 1 of the program, we learn the basics of technology (for example, how to type correctly, how to use Microsoft documents, etc.)

7th Grade ATM

The 7th grade marks the second year of the ATM program, focusing on graphic design. Students are taught to use programs such as Adobe PhotoShop, Illustrator, SketchUp, and FloorPlanner.

8th Grade ATM

The 8th Grade marke the third and final year of the ATM program. Year 3 of the program focuses on multi-media. Students are taught to use programs such as GarageBand, iMovie, and Visual Studio Code.

Reflection

Overall, Bancroft's ATM program is incredible. It is, by far, one of the greatest experiences I've had in my life. The 3-year computer program helps teach technological skills such as typing, coding, filming, etc. Additionally, the ATM program also teaches other skills such as presenting to large groups, floorplanning, and graphic design. The ATM program is the thing that taught me how to code this website from scratch! The phenomenal ATM program has taught me tons of things over the course of 3 years and I am incredibly happy that I was chosen to be in it.
